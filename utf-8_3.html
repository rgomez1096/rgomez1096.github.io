What Languages Does UTF-8 Support?

  • Does UTF-8 Support every language?

    UTF-8 is just a transformation of Unicode. It can turn arbitrary Unicode characters into bytes, even characters that have no meaning yet because the Unicode Consortium has not yet assigned a meaning to them. UTF-8 does not care about the meaning of the characters it encodes. So I think the question really is, ‘Does Unicode support all languages?’. And the answer to that is no. It supports very many languages, and the number of supported languages keeps growing with each new edition of the Unicode standard. However, even now, there are many characters in the Pipeline that have not been added yet, and some of those characters are needed for additional languages (such as Tangut). The ‘most important’ languages have been encoded, for some values of ‘important’ that include considerations such as ‘spoken by many people and widely written in the 21st century’.
